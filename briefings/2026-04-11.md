Concurrently, NERC confirmed it was actively monitoring the electric grid in response to the Iran-linked threat, underscoring cross-sector concern about ICS/SCADA vulnerability. On the policy front, the Trump administration's FY2027 budget proposal — released April 7 and advancing through legislative review — calls for a net $360 million reduction to CISA's budget and the elimination of roughly 867 positions, including the complete elimination of CISA's election security program and its support to state and local election officials. This proposal drew renewed criticism from cybersecurity advocates who argue the cuts compound workforce losses CISA has already sustained, with nearly 1,000 departures recorded since January 2025. The convergence of an active nation-state ICS threat campaign and continued degradation of the federal cyber defense workforce represents one of the most acute domestic security tensions in the current period.

The Kremlin explicitly rejected any extension of the Easter truce into a lasting ceasefire, framing it as a limited humanitarian gesture rather than a diplomatic opening.
