## China / Taiwan

On May 1, 2026, Taiwan's Ministry of National Defense reported detecting four sorties of Chinese military aircraft, five PLAN vessels, and two official ships operating around its territory, with all four sorties entering the southwestern sector of Taiwan's Air Defense Identification Zone (ADIZ). The activity reflects a broader PLA operational tempo tied to the ongoing Balikatan 2026 multinational exercises in the Philippines (April 20–May 8), which this year include record participation from Japan alongside U.S., Australian, and other allied forces. In response to Balikatan, the PLA Southern Theater Command deployed a surface task group—comprising a Type 055 destroyer, Type 052D destroyer, Type 054A frigate, and Type 903A replenishment vessel—to conduct exercises east of the Luzon Strait, a posture analysts assess as signaling PLA capacity to operate beyond the first island chain. The PLAN aircraft carrier Liaoning had earlier transited the Taiwan Strait southbound on April 20, a movement the American Enterprise Institute characterized as part of a broader strategic design to signal resolve to Japan and shape the military balance ahead of potential U.S.-China diplomacy. The China Coast Guard also conducted three separate incursions into waters near Taiwan's Kinmen islands and one near Pratas Island throughout April. AEI analysts note that while absolute ADIZ sortie counts have moderated compared to late 2025 peaks, the PRC appears to be calibrating coercive pressure—preserving ADIZ incursions as a political signaling tool while redirecting PLA resources toward joint combat operations training and modernization.
