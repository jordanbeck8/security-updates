## Domestic US Security

On May 6, 2026, CISA unveiled "CI Fortify," a new initiative directing critical infrastructure operators across all sectors — including water utilities, transportation, dams, radar systems, and defense-connected facilities — to develop plans for operating in full network isolation for "weeks to months" during a geopolitical conflict or cyberattack. CISA Director Nick Andersen stated the guidance is driven by persistent nation-state actors, particularly Chinese-linked Volt Typhoon, which remains pre-positioned inside U.S. critical infrastructure networks despite years of eradication efforts, and by 2026 Russian cyberattacks on operational technology networks in Poland. The two-pillar strategy emphasizes isolation — proactively disconnecting from third-party vendors, internet, and telecommunications — and recovery, including manual operations planning, OT segmentation, and documented backup procedures. CISA has begun pilot assessments with participating organizations and has approval for 329 new mission-critical hires after suffering roughly 1,000 staff cuts and a 75-day DHS operational disruption earlier this year. The announcement coincided with a Defense Department audit finding the Navy made minimal progress mitigating cyber vulnerabilities in defense critical infrastructure systems, underscoring the urgency of the broader federal push. Cybersecurity experts noted that deep adversary entrenchment in OT networks makes segmentation and resilience the most viable defensive posture when full eviction is not achievable.
