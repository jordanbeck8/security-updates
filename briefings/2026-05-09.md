## China / Taiwan

The activity coincided with intense diplomatic maneuvering ahead of the May 14–15 Trump-Xi summit in Beijing, at which China has publicly declared Taiwan its top agenda item, marking a significant departure from the bilateral tone of the prior year's meeting. Compounding Taipei's anxieties, Taiwan's Legislative Yuan passed a supplementary defense budget on May 8 capped at NT$780 billion (approximately $24.8 billion USD) — only two-thirds of what President Lai Ching-te had requested — with opposition parties stripping out funding for domestically developed drones, missiles, and the Chiang Kung interceptor central to Taiwan's planned T-Dome air defense system. The U.S. State Department publicly characterized the legislative cuts and delays as a "concession" to Beijing, warning that the capability gaps — particularly in missile defense — could meaningfully degrade Taiwan's combat effectiveness. Washington's rebuke reflects broader concerns that Taipei's internal political divisions are undermining deterrence at a moment when PRC officials are actively pressing Trump to endorse language on "peaceful unification" before or during the summit. Analysts at CSIS and the Center for American Progress have warned that ambiguous U.S. signals on Taiwan's status, combined with the budget shortfall and ongoing PLA gray-zone operations, present a compounding risk to cross-strait stability in the near term.

## US / Iran
