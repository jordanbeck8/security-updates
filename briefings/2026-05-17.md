## Domestic US Security

CISA's recently launched "CI Fortify" initiative continued to draw implementation attention across the critical infrastructure community in the days surrounding May 17, 2026. Announced in early May, the initiative directs water utilities, transportation operators, energy providers, and other critical infrastructure sectors to prepare contingency plans for geopolitical conflict scenarios in which adversaries simultaneously target operational technology (OT) networks while degrading telecommunications and internet connectivity. Under CI Fortify's framework, operators are instructed to assume that third-party connections — including vendors, service providers, and upstream ISPs — will be unreliable during a conflict, and that threat actors may already have some foothold in OT networks. CISA has signaled it will conduct targeted assessments of how prepared designated entities are, with a prioritized focus on defense-critical infrastructure such as dams, radar systems, weapon systems, and satellite communications. CISA has also been working to rebuild its workforce after losing approximately one-third of staff to cuts and a 75-day shutdown, with 329 mission-critical hires recently approved.

## Russia / Ukraine

Ukraine launched one of its largest-ever drone strikes on Russia overnight May 16–17, sending over 1,050 drones across more than a dozen Russian regions, including Moscow Oblast. Russia's Defense Ministry reported intercepting approximately 1,000 drones, but the attack still killed at least three people near Moscow and one in the Belgorod region, injuring a dozen more. Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin confirmed debris struck near the city's oil refinery and caused damage to several residential buildings, while drone debris also fell on the grounds of Sheremetyevo Airport without disrupting flights. President Zelenskyy confirmed Ukraine was behind the strikes, calling them a "completely fair response" to Russia's ongoing attacks on Ukrainian civilians and framing the operation — jointly carried out by the SBU and the Armed Forces — as targeting military-industrial infrastructure, fuel facilities, and air defense sites including the Belbek airfield in occupied Crimea. On the ground front lines, 195+ combat engagements were recorded across the Pokrovsk and Huliaipole sectors, with Russia continuing heavy use of guided aerial bombs and kamikaze drones; Ukrainian forces reported repelling multiple Russian advances in the Lyman, Yampil, and Stepove areas. The large-scale drone strike underscores Ukraine's growing long-range strike capacity and comes amid stalled ceasefire negotiations, with Russia's territorial demands and Ukraine's insistence on freezing the current front line remaining the core obstacles to any agreement.
