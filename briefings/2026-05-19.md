## Domestic US Security

The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) continued rolling out its newly announced "CI Fortify" initiative in mid-May 2026, directing critical infrastructure owners and operators — including water utilities, energy providers, and transportation sectors — to develop plans for operating in isolation from internet and telecommunications networks during a geopolitical crisis or sophisticated cyberattack targeting operational technology. The initiative, announced May 5, establishes two core planning objectives — "isolation" (proactively disconnecting OT systems from third-party networks) and "recovery" (documenting systems, backing up files, and practicing manual operations fallback) — and explicitly assumes adversaries may already have some degree of access to OT networks, a posture driven in part by persistent Chinese state-sponsored intrusions detected across multiple critical infrastructure sectors. These developments come against a backdrop of significant institutional strain at CISA: DHS Secretary Markwayne Mullin acknowledged the agency lost approximately 1,100 employees — nearly half its pre-shutdown workforce of roughly 2,400 — during the 76-day partial government shutdown that ran from February 14 through April 30. Mullin has approved plans for 329 "mission-critical" hires to begin rebuilding capacity, but officials have warned that cyber partnership programs remain at a near "standstill" amid the staffing deficit. The compounding pressure of workforce attrition alongside elevated nation-state threat activity has drawn congressional scrutiny, with lawmakers pressing Mullin on a timeline for restoring CISA's operational footing. CISA says it will conduct targeted assessments of critical infrastructure organizations' readiness to meet CI Fortify objectives in the coming months.
