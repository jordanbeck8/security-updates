## Domestic US Security

CISA's newly launched "CI Fortify" initiative remained a dominant domestic security story through May 24, with the agency continuing to push critical infrastructure operators — including water utilities, transportation networks, and defense-critical facilities — to plan for geopolitical-crisis cyber scenarios where internet and telecommunications connections could be severed by nation-state actors. The initiative centers on two core planning pillars: "isolation" (proactively disconnecting operational technology from third-party and business networks before or during a conflict) and "recovery" (documenting systems, maintaining offline backups, and rehearsing transitions to manual operations). CISA announced it will conduct targeted assessments of how prepared high-priority operators are to meet CI Fortify objectives, with defense-critical infrastructure — including dams, radar systems, weapon systems, and satellite communications — designated as the first tranche for review.
