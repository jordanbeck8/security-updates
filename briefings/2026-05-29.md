## US / Iran

U.S. and Iranian negotiators reached a tentative 60-day memorandum of understanding on May 28 to extend the ongoing ceasefire and open formal nuclear talks, though President Trump had not signed the agreement as of May 29 and stated he needed "a couple of days to think about it." The proposed framework would bar Iran from imposing tolls on the Strait of Hormuz and require removal of all mines within 30 days, while the U.S. would proportionally lift its naval blockade and relax sanctions to permit Iranian oil sales; nuclear negotiations would focus on Iran's 440.9 kilograms of uranium enriched to 60% purity. VP JD Vance characterized the ceasefire as "always a little messy" but "very much holding," even as CENTCOM formally designated an IRGC ballistic missile strike on Ali Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait — on the night of May 27-28 — as an "egregious ceasefire violation," the first documented Iranian ballistic missile attack on a U.S. Gulf installation since the April 8 ceasefire began. Kuwaiti air defenses successfully intercepted the missile and accompanying drones, with only two minor shrapnel injuries reported. CENTCOM also conducted defensive strikes near Bandar Abbas during the same window, downing five Iranian one-way attack drones near the Strait of Hormuz and destroying a ground control station preparing to launch a sixth, actions the Pentagon described as "measured, purely defensive, and intended to maintain the ceasefire." Houthi Red Sea activity remained minimal in the May 28-29 window, with no confirmed major maritime attacks reported.
