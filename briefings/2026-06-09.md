## China / Taiwan

On June 9, Taiwan's Army 10th Corps conducted a large-scale live-fire coastal defense exercise at the Dajia River estuary in Taichung, simulating the destruction of a Chinese amphibious assault force. The drill fired 372 rounds across a 20-kilometer front, employing Thunderbolt-2000 multiple launch rocket systems — their first operational-area deployment in seven years — alongside M109A2 self-propelled howitzers, TOW-2A/B anti-tank missiles, and 120mm mortars. Military officials stated the exercise validated the unit's kill chain effectiveness and its ability to delay and attrite invading forces at likely PLA landing beaches on Taiwan's west coast. Against this backdrop, the PLA conducted 217 ADIZ incursions in May — a slight month-over-month uptick from April but markedly lower than the 300-plus monthly average recorded in the year following President Lai Ching-te's 2024 election, a pattern analysts assess reflects deliberate unpredictability rather than de-escalation. China's Coast Guard also carried out four incursions into Taiwan-administered waters near Kinmen in May, while Taiwan's Defense Minister Wellington Koo publicly labeled recent Chinese coast guard activity east of Taiwan provocative. The broader strategic context, per AEI's June 5 assessment, is that Beijing continues to normalize gray-zone pressure on Taiwan while prioritizing the East China Sea and South China Sea as higher-priority theaters for active military deterrence.
